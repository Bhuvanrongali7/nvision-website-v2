Checklists

Project Readiness Checklists

⚙️ ERP Implementation Readiness

  • Executive sponsor identified and engaged
  • Business requirements documented and signed off
  • Current state processes mapped end-to-end
  • Data quality assessment completed
  • Change management plan in place
  • Budget and timeline defined and approved
  • Key subject matter experts identified per module
  • Go-live support model agreed upon

☁️ Cloud Migration Readiness

  • Current infrastructure inventory documented
  • Data sensitivity and compliance requirements assessed
  • Customizations catalogued and migration approach defined
  • Integration points with third-party systems identified
  • Network and bandwidth requirements evaluated
  • Security and access controls reviewed
  • Rollback plan documented
  • User training plan prepared

📊 BI Project Readiness

  • Key business questions defined by stakeholder group
  • Data sources identified and access confirmed
  • Data quality issues documented
  • Report ownership and refresh frequency defined
  • Security and row-level access requirements clear
  • Training plan for end users prepared
  • Success metrics agreed upon upfront

🏛️ Government Contract Readiness

  • SAM.gov registration active and current
  • UEI and CAGE code confirmed
  • Relevant NAICS codes identified
  • Contract vehicle selected (GSA MAS or STARS III)
  • Past performance references documented
  • Small business certifications current
  • ISO 9001 or equivalent QMS in place
FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a typical Oracle ERP implementation take? +
Most mid-size implementations run 6–12 months from kickoff to go-live. Fortune 500 or multi-module rollouts can take 12–24 months. The biggest variable is organizational readiness — companies with well-documented processes go live faster.
What's the difference between Oracle EBS and Oracle Cloud? +
Oracle E-Business Suite (EBS) is Oracle's on-premise ERP platform. Oracle Cloud (Fusion) is the cloud-based successor. EBS is still widely supported, but Oracle is pushing customers toward Cloud. We support both and can help you evaluate the right path.
How do you price your engagements? +
We offer both fixed-price and time-and-materials engagements depending on the project. For well-defined implementations, we prefer fixed-price so clients have cost certainty. In all cases, we define scope and budget upfront.
Do you work with government agencies directly? +
Yes. NVision IT holds active GSA MAS (GS-35F-414DA) and 8(a) STARS III (47QTCB22D0369) contract vehicles. Federal agencies can procure our services directly without a competitive RFP. Visit our Public Sector page for full details.
How does your staff augmentation vetting work? +
Every consultant goes through five stages: resume review, preliminary phone screening, technical interview, interpersonal skills assessment, and reference checks. We only submit candidates we would stake our own reputation on.
Can you rescue a failing Oracle implementation? +
Absolutely. We start with a rapid assessment to understand what happened, the current state, and what it takes to stabilize and move forward. Contact us to discuss your situation.
What industries do you serve? +
Defense, Healthcare, Manufacturing, Technology, Telecom, Utilities, Transportation, Finance, Education, and Federal Government. Our Oracle expertise translates across industries because the underlying platform challenges are consistent.
